November 18th, 2016
RE: Encryption and Internet Security

Dear Technology Leaders,

Private companies are critical linchpins in the current apparatus of mass surveillance. Laws like the Electronic Communications Privacy Act, the USA PATRIOT Act, and the FISA Amendments Act of 2008 force companies to search and hand over information they hold on their users’ behalf, independent of the constitutional and international rights of those users. Some of this information is required, by law, to be retained by companies, but much of it is not. Sadly, some companies have developed business models around providing surveillance beyond the scope of what is appropriate and required by law.

As America steps toward President-elect Trump’s time leading this country, his campaign promises of mass persecution and discriminatory surveillance cannot be forgotten. The right to privacy has never been more important—or more imperilled.

You are the frontline of this fight. You did not ask to be, but mass surveillance as it functions today depends not only on the aggression of the government, but also on your cooperation. Your users, especially those whom candidate Trump has promised to surveil, cannot afford to use your services or support your company in the absence of a commitment to protect their privacy.

Accordingly, we call on you to:

  1. Retain information only to the extent that it is legally required;
  2. Purge all information that you are not required to retain by law that is not necessary to provide your products and services;
  3. Pledge to fight overly broad, questionable, and illegal efforts to surveil your users, in the courts and in public;
  4. Implement robust, publicly auditable encryption, so that both legal and illegal efforts to circumvent your role, or to force your cooperation in spite of the law, do not endanger your users; and
  5. Hardware vendors: take endpoint security seriously and only sell Internet-connected devices that receive regular critical security updates to protect users from foreign hackers and identity thieves.

We celebrate the commitment of companies that have taken these steps already, which have proven that ensuring user privacy is not only possible, but wise. We urge you to join them, and us, as we continue the fight against warrantless, mass, and discriminatory surveillance.

Sincerely,

… and 16,384 action takers.
